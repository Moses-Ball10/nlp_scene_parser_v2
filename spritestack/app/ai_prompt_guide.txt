SPRITESTACK STUDIO — AI GENERATION PROMPT GUIDE
================================================

PROMPT STRUCTURE
----------------
A strong prompt has three parts:

  [Subject] + [Style modifiers] + [Palette / lighting hint]

Examples:
  "worn knight armour, dark fantasy, muted blues and browns"
  "wooden treasure chest, outlined pixel art, warm candlelight"
  "stone dungeon wall tile, dithered shading, grey and mossy green"
  "cartoon cloud, cel shaded, bright pastel palette"


STYLE MODIFIER TAGS (click tags in the panel to add them)
----------------------------------------------------------
  outlined       — hard black pixel border around the shape
  dithered       — checkerboard pattern for gradient simulation
  cel shaded     — flat fills with a single hard shadow pass
  pixel outline  — thin 1-pixel crisp outline
  metallic       — reflective highlight + dark edge shading
  worn           — scratches, chips, worn surface detail
  top-down       — overhead perspective
  isometric      — classic 45° angled view
  dark fantasy   — desaturated dark palette with accent highlights
  bright palette — saturated, high contrast colours


SELECTION-BASED GENERATION
---------------------------
1. Make a selection with S (rectangle), A (lasso), or W (magic wand).
2. Switch the mode tab to "Fill Selection" or "Inpaint Region".
   - Fill Selection: generates only inside the selection boundary.
   - Inpaint Region: sends surrounding context pixels too — better
     for blending seamlessly into existing art.
3. Choose your Output Mode:
   - New layer       — safest; result is on its own layer.
   - Replace selection — writes pixels directly into the active layer.
   - Blend into layer — writes at 70% opacity; original shows through.


STRENGTH SETTING
----------------
  1–3  Subtle: slight colour/texture shift, original shape preserved.
  4–6  Moderate: clear changes, general shapes retained.
  7–10 Strong: near-full repaint of the region.


PALETTE LOCK
------------
Forces output pixels to snap to colours already present in the
active layer. Prevents new colours entering the palette.
Best used when you have a strict colour budget for your sprite sheet.


TIPS
----
• Undo (Ctrl+Z) rolls back any AI generation.
• Use Variations = 2 or 4 to get multiple options at once.
• Describe what you DON'T want if the output keeps repeating an
  unwanted feature, e.g. "no blue tint, no glow".
